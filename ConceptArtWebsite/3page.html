Environment Concept Art

Environments in Animation and Film

Concept art for Spirited Away by Hayao Miyazaki.

Environment artists working in film and animation face a unique challenge: their designs must support storytelling within a designated time frame. Unlike video games, where a player might explore vast areas, films typically focus on isolated scenes. As a result, environmental concept artists are often limited to designing specific angles or compositions for a setting.

In many cases, only one or two perspectives of a given scene are shown on screen. This constraint demands that environment artists create striking and representative visuals that effectively convey mood, narrative, and detail within a single composition. Efficiency is key, as these artists must ensure that each scene captivates the audience while serving its purpose in the story.

Environments in Video Games

Concept art for Call of Duty Cold War by Sam J. Chon.

Environment artists working with video game studios experience a significantly different creative process. Instead of designing isolated scenes, they must craft expansive, interconnected areas that players can freely explore. Modern video games often operate in three-dimensional spaces, requiring compositions that adapt seamlessly to player movement and perspective.

To guide players, artists may strategically de-emphasize certain areas while drawing attention to critical objectives or pathways. This can be achieved through lighting, color, and level design principles. However, video game environments must still uphold the same expectations as those in film—they need to be representative of the game’s world, consistent with its narrative, and visually engaging throughout.

Whether creating sprawling open worlds or more confined settings, environment artists in video games balance functionality with artistry, ensuring that every area is immersive, purposeful, and aesthetically pleasing.
