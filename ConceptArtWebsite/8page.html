Character Design Techniques

Mastering Anatomy, Poses, and Gestures

Study of arms by Naranbaatar Ganbold

A compelling character design begins with a strong grasp of anatomy, poses, and gestures. Anatomy ensures that characters feel physically grounded, whether they’re realistic or stylized. Poses convey personality and action, telling a story about the character’s mood, role, or intent. Gestures add dynamism and emotion, making characters relatable and memorable.

Costume Design

Base Viktor Visual Update by Rudy Siswanto

Costume design plays a key role in defining a character's identity. By drawing from cultural influences, artists can create costumes that feel authentic to the character's background. For instance, a warrior from a desert region might wear light, flowing fabrics, while a futuristic soldier might sport sleek, tech-inspired armor. Costumes often include functional details, like pockets, straps, or insignias, to reflect the character's profession or status.

Facial Expressions and Body Language

Character Designs for S1 of Jurassic World Chaos Theory by William Nichols

Facial expressions and body language are the final layer of emotional storytelling in character design. A smirk, furrowed brow, or subtle slouch can communicate volumes about a character’s personality or inner conflict. By combining these visual cues, artists create characters that resonate with audiences and leave a lasting impression.
