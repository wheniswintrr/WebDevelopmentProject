Tools of the Trade

Common Tools

Traditional concept art drawings by Nova.

Concept art is a field where both traditional and digital tools play significant roles. Traditional tools, such as pencils, markers, and paints, are indispensable for capturing raw ideas quickly and intuitively. Many artists prefer the tactile feel of these tools when creating loose sketches or exploring color schemes. Watercolors and gouache are also favored for their ability to produce organic textures and dynamic hues.

Digital Standards

Digital Rendering for Arcane, 2021, by Thibaut Granet.

In the digital realm, tools like Adobe Photoshop and Procreate are industry standards, offering versatile features for creating detailed and dynamic artwork. Photoshop excels in photobashing, layering, and advanced rendering, while Procreate is popular for its user-friendly interface and adaptability on tablets like the iPad. Blender, a 3D modeling software, is increasingly used for designing environments and characters, providing realistic perspectives and lighting.

Hardware Essentials

Drawing on Tablet by Josh Art.

When it comes to hardware, a reliable drawing tablet is essential. Wacom and Huion tablets are commonly used for their precision and compatibility, while all-in-one devices like the iPad Pro provide portability and versatility. High-performance PCs with robust GPUs are also crucial for handling resource-intensive applications like 3D modeling software, ensuring smooth and efficient workflows.
